üí° KILLER FAQs (Objection Handling)

Q: Where are the company's private keys?

A: The company (the Gnosis Safe) DOES NOT have a private key. It is a smart contract, pure code.

It only obeys the keys of the signers (Alice, Bob, Charlie). This eliminates the risk of "losing the company master key." There is no such master key.

Q: And where are Alice, Bob, and Charlie's keys?

A: They are secured by Magic.link using HSM (Hardware Security Modules) and Multi-Party Computation.

Translation: The keys are fragmented and encrypted on ultra-secure servers.

Alice only accesses her "permission to use" by logging into Trustyfy. Alice never sees her private key (nor does she need to), which prevents it from being stolen via phishing.

Q: If Trustyfy gets hacked, do they take my money?

A: No. Trustyfy is just the window (the interface).

If Trustyfy disappears tomorrow, your money remains on the blockchain (in the Gnosis Safe).

You could interact with that Gnosis Safe using another interface (like the official Gnosis app) using the same Magic.link credentials. Trustyfy does not have custody of your funds.

Q: What happens if Bob loses access to his email?

A: Since the policy is 2/3, Alice and Charlie can vote to replace Bob with a new signer (David).

The Vault allows changing the locks without having to move the funds to a new account. Funds stay exactly where they are.
