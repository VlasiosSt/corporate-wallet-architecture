4.1 What is a Smart Wallet and How it Works

Trustyfy's corporate accounts utilize smart contract wallets identical to Gnosis Safe architecture. These are NOT standard wallets with private keys. Instead, they are smart contracts deployed on the blockchain, commonly referred to as "smart wallets."

üîë THE GOLDEN RULE

Corporate wallets are NOT standard wallets with private keys

They are smart contracts deployed on-chain with RULES, not keys. This eliminates the risk of "losing the master key."

Smart Wallet Technology Enables:

Multi-signature functionality

Require multiple approvals for transactions

Programmable access control

Define roles and permissions programmatically

Gasless transactions

End users never pay gas fees

Enhanced security

Distributed authorization prevents single points of failure

üèõÔ∏è Battle-Tested Technology

Corporate wallets inherit the security properties of Gnosis Safe architecture‚Äîwidely adopted and trusted by major crypto institutions holding billions in assets. Smart contract-based wallets cannot be compromised through private key theft.

4.2 Addresses Per Chain and Risks

‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL: Cross-Chain Address Variability üö®üö®üö®

Unlike personal wallets (which have a single private key), corporate smart wallets have DIFFERENT ADDRESSES on DIFFERENT BLOCKCHAINS

Ethereum (ETH): 0x7a2b3c4d...

Polygon (POL): 0x4f5e6d7c...

BSC: 0x9d87b61...

‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL WARNING FOR USERS:

If a user sends funds to the wrong chain address, THE FUNDS WILL BE PERMANENTLY LOST AND CANNOT BE RECOVERED. Always verify you are using the correct address for the specific blockchain you are transacting on.

üìå Key Difference from Personal Wallets

Personal wallets (with private keys) maintain the same address across all chains because they derive from a single private key.

Smart wallets are deployed contracts, so each blockchain deployment results in a different contract address. This is a fundamental property of smart contract wallets and cannot be changed.

5. Multisig and Approval Policies

5.1 What is M/N?

The approval policy defines the number of signatures required to execute a transaction from a corporate wallet. This is commonly referred to as a "multisig threshold" or M/N configuration.

Understanding the M/N Format

M

Required Approvals

The minimum number of signatures needed to execute a transaction

N

Total Signers

The total number of admin signers authorized on the wallet

üìå Simple Example

A 2/3 approval policy means:

  • ‚Ä¢ There are 3 admin signers total on the wallet
  • ‚Ä¢ 2 signatures are required to execute any transaction
  • ‚Ä¢ Any 2 of the 3 admins can approve to move funds
  • ‚Ä¢ The transaction won't execute until 2 approvals are collected

  • ‚Ä¢ The same admin who proposed can also be one of the approvers (counts toward the threshold)
  • ‚Ä¢ Signatures are cryptographically verified on-chain

5.5 Best Practices by Company Size

Choosing the right approval policy depends on your company size, operational needs, and risk tolerance. Here are recommended configurations:

Solo Founders / Very Small Teams (1-2 people)

Recommended: 1/1 or 1/2

Single signature for speed, or 1/2 if you have a trusted co-founder as backup

Rationale:

Prioritize operational speed over security for small amounts. Can upgrade later as you scale.

Small Business (3-10 people)

Recommended: 2/3 or 2/4

Dual approval balances security with operational efficiency

Rationale:

Prevents single-point-of-failure while maintaining fast approval cycles. Standard for most businesses.

Medium to Large Enterprises (10+ people)

Recommended: 3/5 or higher

Majority approval for maximum security and governance

Rationale:

Enterprise-grade security for high-value assets. Requires consensus from leadership team.

üìö Glossary of Terms

Payment Methods:

SEPA (Single Euro Payments Area)

Standard European bank transfer system for EUR payments. Typically processed within 1-2 business days. Widely accepted across Europe.

SEPA Instant

Real-time EUR transfers within the SEPA zone, completed in seconds. Available 24/7 including weekends and holidays.

ACH (Automated Clearing House)

US-based electronic bank transfer network. Typically processed within 1-3 business days.

Wire Transfer

Direct bank-to-bank electronic transfer. Faster than ACH (same or next business day). Available domestically and internationally.

SWIFT

International wire transfer network used for cross-border USD and other currency transfers. Standard method for global payments.

Payment Types:

First-Party Payment

Payments where the sender and account holder are the same legal entity. The company can only receive funds from accounts in their own name.

Third-Party Payment

Payments involving different parties. The company can receive funds from (Pay In) or send funds to (Pay Out) accounts not in their own name, such as customers, suppliers, or partners.
