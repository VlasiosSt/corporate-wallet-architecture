1. Introduction

1.1 What is a Corporate Account?

A Trustyfy Corporate Account is an enterprise-grade financial platform that combines traditional banking with blockchain technology, designed specifically for businesses that need to manage both fiat currencies and cryptocurrencies.

Unlike traditional bank accounts or basic crypto wallets, a Corporate Account provides:

Multi-signature Security

Built-in approval workflows requiring multiple team members to authorize transactions

Dual Currency Support

Seamlessly manage EUR, USD, and major cryptocurrencies in one platform

Team Collaboration

Role-based permissions, spending limits, and activity auditing

Enterprise Compliance

Full KYB verification, audit trails, and regulatory compliance

In simple terms: Think of it as your company's financial command center where traditional banking meets the future of digital assets, with built-in safeguards to protect your business.

üìö Glossary of Terms

Payment Methods:

SEPA (Single Euro Payments Area)

Standard European bank transfer system for EUR payments. Typically processed within 1-2 business days. Widely accepted across Europe.

SEPA Instant

Real-time EUR transfers within the SEPA zone, completed in seconds. Available 24/7 including weekends and holidays.

ACH (Automated Clearing House)

US-based electronic bank transfer network. Typically processed within 1-3 business days.

Wire Transfer

Direct bank-to-bank electronic transfer. Faster than ACH (same or next business day). Available domestically and internationally.

SWIFT

International wire transfer network used for cross-border USD and other currency transfers. Standard method for global payments.

Payment Types:

First-Party Payment

Payments where the sender and account holder are the same legal entity. The company can only receive funds from accounts in their own name.

Third-Party Payment

Payments involving different parties. The company can receive funds from (Pay In) or send funds to (Pay Out) accounts not in their own name, such as customers, suppliers, or partners.
